Bear with Me

Maker Faire: Bear with Me

"The Bear with Me project isn’t just a terrible pun. While in Canada, I saw a family of bears crossing a river and it brought me to an analogy with the project on propulsion I had started. I have authentic footage of this natural phenomenon and would play it given the opportunity at the fair. Though the bears need some form of propulsion to get across the river, we need some form of propulsion to get across space. But space travel is limited by the weight that has to go with the spacecraft. A significant portion of this weight is fuel. The bears have a similar problem. Without enough thrust to get across the river, they are dead, as are we. In order to make space travel more efficient, we need to limit our spacecrafts to having the bear necessities and be able to explore space with as little weight as possible. This project takes forms of propulsion, mainly the EmDrive that enable spacecrafts to operate at a much lesser weight and higher efficiency, and assesses their worth for investment and production for the future. As the bears cross the river, we need to cross the vast expanse of space."

The Maker Faire is an event that is just what it sounds like: a fair in which makers come together. Whether it be a completely new facet of psychology that can be perceived in a model or a presentation on the newest kind of household robot, people who have ideas come together to input their ideas. As a single strand of spaghetti is easy to break, thousands held together are not - and the same principle applies here. It's not just something that allows you to present your own idea, but to interact with others as well. When we come together as a group and share ideas, it is much stronger than our individual ideas kept to ourselves.

Though I won't reveal what my project will include, its main concept comes from the analogy between the two bear cubs just barely being able to cross the river and us possibly not being able to cross the sea of space. Like the bears, we are in a tough situation in which we have to act fast. Arguably, we have a little more time than the 30 seconds to cross the river but is still nonetheless, very short. So with the time we have, we need to find the best ideas, the most insane concepts, in order to be able to cross the river. We won't be able to get better with basic progression on something that already exists. Maybe we can expand on something that already exists, but we have to think insane. The smartest people in all of history were often thought to be insane for their means of thinking, and we just have to emulate that, hoping that we will be able to breach the heavens, as they did.
