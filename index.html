Pierce the Heavens Mission

The Pierce the Heavens Project aims to investigate the rapid rise of revolutionary technologies, most of which are unpredictable or ignored by the general populace. These include artificial intelligence, new means of space propulsion, and new philosophies like bioethics. The force behind new technologies being developed at such a fast pace is relentless human innovation. As the potential of novel inventions are realized, they are tied into the metaphysical application into our lives. The concept of the impossible is a significant source of inspiration for myself, and our telos to reason never fails to prove my faith in continued, or even infinite human prosperity.

My Inspiration

Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann is a 27-episode mecha anime made in 2007 by Gainax, directed by Hiroyuki Imaishi. Its themes of never giving up, doing the impossible, and believing in oneself were presented in amazing animation, backed by an epic soundtrack, and held with a very interesting plot. There are many subtle references to space travel in the anime, so when I began my project on improving propulsion, just one year later, the connection immediately struck me. I began to build everything that I did by incorporating the series motifs and main ideas, so here is a video that I found that best sums up my inspiration for this project (SPOILERS).
