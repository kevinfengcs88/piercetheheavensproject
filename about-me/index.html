About Me

 

Hi, my name is Kevin Feng, and I am the creator of the Pierce the Heavens Project. I enjoy playing piano, researching the future of human innovation, pondering about what is philosophical, and finding answers to metaphysical questions. I have found that ideals are far more captivating than simple ideas, though they both often "have" people rather than the other way around. Ideals are expressed all around us, especially in books, shows, and most notably, Japanese animation.

There is an astounding correlation between thinkers that seek the ideal outcome for humanity (for example, Elon Musk) and their affinity towards thematic messages supporting anime. I believe it's due to the numerous ideals presented by Japanese culture, often extending past what is tangible. My primary source of inspiration comes from "Tengen Toppa Gurren Lagann," a mecha anime created by Studio Gainax in 2007.

For me, the show perfectly represents the limits of human innovation: none. With references to other influential shows and even the Epic of Gilgamesh from 2000 BC, Gurren Lagann crafts a story that presents the inevitable maturation of everyone and the ability to do the impossible. The telos of human beings to reason has further inspired me to take on the impossible and look deeper into the rising technologies of artificial intelligence, advanced spacecraft propulsion, and many more.

My Story

Though a large part of my mindset and inspiration comes from Gurren Lagann, I have been writing my own story as well. When I  began to write my story, I realized that everything was in permanent ink, there was no going back. Some things could be fixed, but only by continuing in the story, writing something new, to allow for the scribbling over of the mistake. With this in mind, I tried to make the most entertaining story possible, with as few mistakes as possible, but of course, there would be many mistakes, many errors that would teach me to make the story better and better. As the plot progresses, the quality and description of my story will increase as well, by learning from previous failures. I would also realize that I could learn from others' mistakes as well, and minimize my own at the same time. Here is my story so far.

When I was very young, I recall spending a lot of time observing nature. I found myself watching ants work on their tunnels in between the crack that still lies between our driveway and garage. Yet even if I cruelly stomped on their progress, they would not cease to work, rebuilding what they had created, knowing it would be destroyed again. It was a very basic example of never giving up, and I don't think I truly understood the meaning of these ants' work ethic and determination.

Later, when I was around eight to ten or so, I enjoyed reading Calvin and Hobbes, and I stumbled across two comic strips in which Calvin faced the same situation I had with the ants:

The connection hit me instantly, and I think the nudge that Calvin and Hobbes gave me was enough to get me to realize the magnitude of the power of never giving up.

Similar to Calvin's relationship to Hobbes, I was influenced significantly by my older brother. We always played games together, and when I woke up in the middle of the night to go to the bathroom or get a drink of water, he would always grant me protection from the monsters in the dark. But I also remember that I got him anything he wanted, and always followed him around. But time flies by at unbelievable speeds, and soon I was losing time at home, going to school.

The first few years of school were really nothing of learning, they were rather there so we could experience the school environment. I feel like nothing really important happened in a short amount of time until middle school. My brother went off to college, and in 8th grade, I took geometry. For one project, we were allowed to choose from a variety of topics. I decided to choose impossible figures for my project, and I wrote a 5 paragraph abstract on impossible figures, as well as drawing what I called the "Impossible Snowflake." I found an image, and I took days to just draw every single line and shade each section.

In my abstract, I mentioned so many concepts of what the impossible really was and my take on if it actually exists. I didn't know it then, but that project would lead me to this website and this research project on spacecraft propulsion among other technologies.

The summer after that year, my brother introduced me to anime. Of course, I knew what it was, but I hadn't watched a series from start to finish. The amount of effort in animation and voice acting in such a short time period is simply amazing to me, and Gurren Lagann was no exception. I specifically remember my brother asking me if I wanted to watch Gurren Lagann or another series that I don't recall the name of. We had watched one episode of Gurren Lagann already, and with that, I was hooked. I don't know if my response telling him I wanted to watch it was driven by my interest from the first episode, or by simple instinct.

Simply put, Gurren Lagann has changed my life like nothing else ever has, and though it may sound dumb or not empowering, yes, an anime has been able to influence my morals, my goals, and how I think through every single situation.

So that's how much I've written in my story so far, and I believe that it can continue to grow into something much greater than it is now.

Purpose of my Website

I created this website as a form of not only showing the research I have done, but my inspiration, and how that has influenced me as a person. I understand that the world is evolving, as taxi cabs are being outcompeted by Uber and Lyft, as more and more people become dependent on using smartphones and electronics for countless functions, and as expectations of new generations are lifted higher and higher. I believe that a website is a simple method to present what I wish to accomplish and my incentive for doing so.

I hope that my website will be able to further technological progress and keep the beast of human curiosity satisfied.
