Learn a Bit About Propulsion

What is propulsion?

Propulsion is the movement or push of something forwards. In space, propulsion comes in many different propulsion systems that are able to generate thrust, to push the spacecraft forwards. In the vacuum of space, and without the gravitational pull of Earth, a spacecraft needs far less thrust than a plane still held by the gravity of Earth. Propulsion is the essential factor of spacecrafts that allow them to move forwards without the assistance of gravity.

How does propulsion work?

There are many forms of propulsion, with a wide variety of systems that are able to generate thrust for movement of spacecrafts:

  1. Chemical rockets - standard fuel since space race; splicing ice from polar caps on Mars into hydrogen and oxygen for rocket fuel
  2. Electrothermal - super-heated plasma is created by electrical energy and pushed through a supersonic nozzle; more ideal for longer missions, as they do not store volatile chemicals
  3. Ion drive - low thrust, but long-lasting propulsion works by ionizing unreactive fuels (primarily xenon)[slow at picking up speed, but may deliver more than 10 times as much thrust as a chemical rocket
  4. Solar sails - photons pushing a solar sail to propel a spacecraft; further away from the sun means less efficient propulsion; tugboat for pulling the Earth away from the sun
  5. Plasma propulsion engine (not yet tested in space) - engine takes magnetic currents and electrical potentials to accelerate plasma to generate thrust
  6. Thermal fission - extremely short travel times to Mars by using conventional fission to heat a propellant and generate thrust for a spacecraft
  7. Continuous fission - compressing electrically charged particles and then accelerating them to the speed of light before they are forced out of the rocket’s propulsion system
  8. Pulsed fusion (testing would violate nuclear test ban treaties) - controlling miniature fission bombs, similar to cars’ control of gasoline explosions
  9. Nanospacecraft - smaller crafts, more easier to propel, instead of increasing propulsion efficiency
  10. Antimatter (highly unlikely) - extremely concentrated fuel, by forcing atomic particles to collide with their antimatter particles; usable amount of energy has not been found [really awesome-sounding…]

Most methods of propulsion follow Newton's Third Law: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

For example, when ion drives control fusion reactions, the huge energy is outputted through the backend of the spacecraft, and therefore, thrust is generated, and the spacecraft leaps forwards. 

What is the EmDrive?

The EmDrive is the main exception to forms of propulsion, as it does not follow Newton's Third Law. It is able to generate thrust without any input of fuel, which is extremely significant, as that can reduce the weight of a spacecraft (fuel taking up a large portion of weight on most spacecrafts today), increasing speed, and efficiency.

Why use the EmDrive?

Not necessarily use it, but rather focus on it, seeing what can be expanded upon and how it can be produced for space usage. It is a significant form of propulsion because it is not a standard, true means of propulsion, requiring no propellant to be carried. This drastically reduces the weight of the spacecraft, thus increasing speed, lowering travel time, and drops costs of maintenance and input for allowing the engine to be functional. Its fast speeds also prove its worth, as it could potentially cut travel time to Mars down to 70 days, just roughly 10 weeks or so.

What is the Hall Thruster?

The Hall Thruster was developed to improve propulsion of smaller satellites. Only the size of a table-top, the XR-100 design uses ejection of plasma as the form of propelling the spacecraft. It is powerful enough to have small satellites orbit, but not enough for the large spacecrafts that carry large amounts of cargo. A Hall thruster works by accelerating the plasma exhaust to extremely high speeds. The process starts with a current of electrons spiraling through a circular channel. On the path down from the negatively-charged exhaust end to the positively-charged inner side of the chamber, the electrons would run into atoms, usually xenon, that is fed into the chamber. Thus, such collisions knock electrons off of the xenon, turning them into positively charged ions. This spiraling motion of the electrons also builds an electrical field powerful enough to pull the gas ions out of the chamber and exhaust end. Enough electrons leave with the ions so that the spacecraft itself does not accumulate a positive charge, resulting in other electrical and circuitry problems. Charged xenon can move at speeds of 30,000 meters per second. Two other systems also have to be perfected in conjunction with the design. Firstly, a converter for the power of the solar sails would be necessary for solar energy to be utilized effectively, as well as the means of feeding xenon gas into the chamber.

How is being linked to solar sails important for the Hall Thruster?

Distance from Earth to Mars is not efficient as traveling towards the solar source, the Sun, but the overall distance from the sun to Mars is not as great as the distance from the Sun to the outer reaches of the solar system. Solar sails essentially limit spacecrafts that use it for the fact that areas in which the spacecraft can be (Sun and its radius) are defined. So if the Hall Thruster were to be travelling away from the Sun, the efficiency which it would run at would gradually decrease, as the source of energy lessens. Not utilizing the Sun as the primary energy source would allow the Hall Thruster to be more efficient in reaches of the solar system that the Sun does not reach as intensively. 

Why is the does the EmDrive has so much significance?

The EmDrive is a piece of technology with such huge potential because it doesn't require the use of fossil fuels, or any nonrenewable fuel that adds weight to the system. This aids space travel most directly, as additional weight to the propulsion system means the spacecraft itself is heavier, making the exit of Earth's gravitational pull more difficult. But this also means due to its electrical input, the EmDrive is able to easily be powered, cleanly, through solar panels, windmills, etc. Through efficient obtaining of clean energy, the EmDrive will eventually be able to power any appliance that requires thrust. Payloads in space can travel very efficiently, terrestrial vehicles such as cars can rely on the thrust, and so many more applications.
